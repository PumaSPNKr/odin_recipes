Takoyaki

Description

Takoyaki are savory Japanese street snacks made of battered, grilled dough balls filled with diced octopus, tempura scraps,
and green onion, cooked in a special molded pan for a crispy exterior and molten center. They’re typically drizzled with tangy sauce, mayo, bonito flakes,
and seaweed, celebrated for their playful texture and Osaka origins.

Ingredients

  • Octopus (tako) – Precooked and diced into tender pieces.
  • Takoyaki batter – A mix of flour, dashi (Japanese stock), eggs, and water.
  • Tenkasu (tempura scraps) – Adds crunch to the filling.
  • Takoyaki sauce – Sweet-savory glaze (similar to Worcestershire or tonkatsu sauce).
  • Toppings – Kewpie mayo, bonito flakes (katsuobushi), and dried seaweed (aonori).

Directions

  1. Mix batter: Combine flour, dashi, egg, and water until smooth.
  2. Fill molds: Pour batter into a greased takoyaki pan; add octopus, tenkasu, and green onion.
  3. Cook: Flip balls with a skewer as edges set, shaping into spheres.
  4. Crisp: Rotate until golden and cooked through.
  5. Serve: Drizzle with takoyaki sauce, mayo, bonito flakes, and aonori. Enjoy hot!
